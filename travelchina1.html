Magical China
Colossal, exuberant and foreign China isnt easily compared to anywhere else on the planet. Home to approximately one fifth of the human race, it variously dazzles, befuddles, frustrates and thrills. The key visitor attractions are renowed around the globe - think the Great Wall, the Forbidden City and the Terracotta Warriors - but the sheer scale and off-kilter energy of the place that leave the most lasting impression.
The ecomonic drive of recent times means many of China's cities are as shaped by modernity as anywhere you care to mention, but it's also somewhere underpinned by dearly held customs and hear-unfathomable amount of diversity. China's landscapes unfurl across the map in vast territory and its sights, sounds nad infinite oddities collectively amount to one of the world's truly great travel experiences. The food's fantastic too, and getting to grip with the different regional cuisines can be hugely enjoyable.
China Cities

Beijing
You will see seamless juxtaposition of modern arts and historical heritages in Beijing, a glamorous yet profound city in the northern China. Apart from the customarily sightseeing Tiananmen Square, Great wall, and forbidden city, the modern construction of water cube(national aquatic center) , the bird's nest (national stadium) rejuvenate the splendid city with exuberant dynamics!

Shanghai
Here is the intersection of western and eastern culture, you will never be surprised by “eavesdropping” multi-language conversation! It is a dream destiny for internationalists in terms of integrated atmosphere, multinational eateries or the magnificent architecture! Western customs and Chinese traditions intertwined and formed the city's culture, modernisation is the buzzword throughout your journey!

Tibet
Tibet enchants tourists from China and abroad with its landscape, religious traditions, culture, and its unsolved mysteries. At any mention of this land, the images of snowy mountains, mirror-like lakes, Potala Palace and Buddhist disciples immediately come to mind. In addition, it is the can’t-miss pilgrimage route for Buddhists!
